Netflix Prize for Predicting Users’ Rating

  • Netflix Prize was open competition held by Netflix, a DVD former rental company, for the best algorithm to predict users’ rating of movies.
  • The grand prize of $1,000,000 was awarded in 2009 to a team composed of researchers from Australia and AT&T lab in New Jersey that came up with a algorithm that reduced the ROOT MEAN SQUARED ERROR by 10%.
  • Key words:
    • Data Mining
    • Big Data
    • Model Assessment

Even lawyers need to know statistics!

  • O. J. Simpson is a former NFL star player and was accused of murdering his ex-wife and her boyfriend. He was ultimately acquitted after a lengthy trial.
  • He had records of abusing his ex-wife. Is this a strong piece of evidence?
  • However the defense were sophisticated enough to cite some statistics: there was only one woman murdered for every 2500 women who were subjected to spousal abuse. \(P(\text{a woman murdered}|\text{subjected to spousal abuse})=1/2500\)
  • What’s the probability that a domestic abuser actually murdered his partner, given that she was subjected to domestic violence and murdered?
  • Keyword:
    • Conditional Probability
    • Bayes Theorem

Our Computational Tool: R

  • R is an open source programming language specifically designed for statistical analysis.
  • Originally for academic research purpose mainly, R has become increasingly popular in industry, even NYTimes ran a profile of it.
  • Our usage of R will be very basic and we will provide detailed instruction. The aim is to expose you to REAL data analysis, not to teach you how to program.
  • The R interface we will be using is RStudio. RStudio provides a nice interface to work with R.

More Notes on R and RStudio

  • R is the back engine that is supposed to be used through command line, and RStudio is the graphical interface that provides better integration and control.
  • You need to install R before trying to install RStudio.
